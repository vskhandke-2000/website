How does prediction take place?

We get the output of an algorithm after it has been trained on a historical dataset and applied to new data when forecasting the likelihood of a particular outcome, such as % of population growth. The algorithm will generate probable values for an unknown variable for each record in the new data, allowing the model builder to identify what that value will most likely be. Random Forest is a popular machine learning algorithm that belongs to the supervised learning technique. It can be used for both Classification and Regression problems in ML. It is based on the concept of ensemble learning, which is a process of combining multiple classifiers to solve a complex problem and to improve the performance of the model which why we have used it in our model too.

Factors

Import

It contains the total amount of goods produced abroad but sold domestically per year in millions.

In dataset it is the total goods imported in that year in millions

Industrial production

It contains the total amount of goods produced domestically and sold abroad per year in millions.

In dataset it is the total goods produced in millions in a year

Inflation rate

Inflation refers to an overall increase in the Consumer Price Index (CPI), which is a weighted average of prices for different goods.

In dataset it is the % of inflation rate increased with respect to previous year

Exchange rate

It is value of one currency for the purpose of conversion to another and in this case USD to Rupees.

In dataset it is the USD to Rupee exchange rate in that year.

Population Growth

Population growth is the increase in the number of individuals in a specific country per year.

In dataset it is the % of population increased compared to previous year.

Unemployment rate

It reflects the inability of an economy to generate employment for those persons who want to work but are not doing so.

In dataset it is the % of unemployed people in that specific year.

Export

It contains the total amount of goods produced domestically and sold abroad per year in millions.

In dataset it is the total goods exported in that year in millions.

Stock market sales

It refers to the collection of markets and exchanges where regular activities of buying,selling,and issuance of shares of companies take place.

In dataset it is the % of stock market sales in that year.

Sensex rate

Sensex is the benchmark index of India's BSE and is comprised of 30 of the largest and most actively-traded stocks.

In dataset it is the Gain or loss of sensex in that specific year.
